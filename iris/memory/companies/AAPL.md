# AAPL analysis refresh
- Sources used: FMP FY2025 annual income statement, balance sheet, cash flow, profile, ratios; Yahoo Finance quotes for AAPL/MSFT/GOOGL/META/AMZN.
- FY2025 revenue $416.2B, gross margin 46.9%, operating expenses 14.9% of revenue, EBIT margin 31.9%, effective tax rate 15.6%, capex 3.1% of revenue, free cash flow $98.8B.
- Balance sheet: cash + short-term investments $54.7B, total debt $112.4B, so net debt is about $57.7B.
- Current price snapshot: $250.32/share; trailing P/E 31.6x, forward P/E 26.9x, EV/EBITDA 24.6x.
- Base-case DCF assumptions: single consolidated revenue segment, 5-year growth 6%, 5%, 4%, 3.5%, 3%; WACC 8.5%; terminal growth 3.0%; working-capital drag 1.2% of revenue.
- Base-case DCF fair value $121.47/share vs current price $250.32, implying -51.5% downside. Implied multiples at fair value: forward P/E 15.3x, EV/EBITDA 13.3x, FCF yield 5.5%.
- Sensitivity range remains broad at $79.79 to $262.35/share; discounted terminal value is about 77% of EV, so valuation is highly assumption-sensitive.
- Comps cross-check via Yahoo Finance: peer forward P/E median for MSFT/GOOGL/META/AMZN is about 21.7x and EV/EBITDA median about 16.7x, while AAPL trades at 26.9x and 24.6x respectively. AAPL gross margin 46.9% is below peer median ~59.7%.
- Current stance: Apple remains a top-tier franchise with exceptional cash generation and ecosystem durability, but at current price the stock appears expensive versus a conservative DCF and also rich versus mega-cap peers. A more constructive view would require evidence of sustained AI/device cycle acceleration, Services mix lift, or materially higher long-term growth than modeled.
